\item Setting aside the particular value $\theta_0 = \arctan(1/2)$ of the inclination angle for the moment, simplify the expression
\[
   r = m(\alpha) = \frac{50(\cot \alpha  - \cot\theta_0)}{\alpha - \theta_0}
\]
for the average rate of change function. Do this by expressing the cotangent function in terms of sine and cosine. Then do some algebra and use a trig identity.  %The result should be
\[
   r = m(\alpha) = \frac{-50\sin(\answer{\alpha - \theta_0})}{(\answer{\sin\alpha \sin\theta_0})(\alpha - \theta_0)} .
\]

\item Use the result of part (h) to find an expression for the scaling factor
\[
  \frac{ds}{d\theta}\Big|_{\theta = \theta_0} = \lim_{\alpha \to \theta_0} \frac{-50\sin(\alpha - \theta_0)}{(\sin\alpha \sin\theta_0)(\alpha - \theta_0)}  .
\]
You'll need to use the fact that
\[
 \lim_{\phi \to 0} \frac{\sin\phi}{\phi} = 1.
\]

The scaling factor is
\begin{align*}
   \frac{ds}{d\theta}\Big|_{\theta = \theta_0}  &= \lim_{\alpha \to \theta_0} \frac{-50\sin(\alpha - \theta_0)}{(\sin\alpha \sin\theta_0)(\alpha - \theta_0)} \\ \\
                                                                    &= -\frac{50}{\sin^2\theta_0}.
\end{align*}
